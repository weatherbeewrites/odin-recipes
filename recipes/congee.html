How to Make

Congee

Congee is regarded as the ultimate Chinese comfort food, according to the author Fuchsia Dunlop. This recipe for ji zhou or chicken congee, from her book on Jiangnan regional cuisine, is dead simple and satisfying. Serve it with chicken and soy sauce for a late-night Shanghai-style snack. —Sara Bonisteel

Congee is a savory rice porridge made by boiling grains in water until soft. It is a staple food in many parts of Asia and can be eaten for breakfast or as a side dish.

Ingredients

    yield:

    3 to 4 servings

    ¾cup/150 grams sushi rice

    2½quarts/2.5 liters chicken stock

    Salt

    Soy sauce, for serving

    Thinly sliced spring onions or scallions, green parts only, for serving

    Finely chopped fresh ginger, for serving

    Sesame oil, for serving

Steps

    Step 1 Rinse and drain the rice.

    Step 2 Put the rice in a pot with the chicken stock and bring to boil

    Step 3 Serve the congee with a sprinkling of soy sauce, spring onion and ginger and a few drops of sesame oil to taste.
